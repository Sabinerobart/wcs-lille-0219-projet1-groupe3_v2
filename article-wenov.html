WENOV, the future of the innovative digital city is at EuraTechnologies

In the largest digital hub
The President of EuraTechnologies, Pierre de Saintignon, is convinced of this: "With the Wenov project, we are sending a strong signal to the tech planet: Euratechnologies, one of Europe's largest start-up campuses, is enriched by a dedicated to R & D and training in the digital professions ".
Developed by Vinci Immobilier in collaboration with the firm Lalou-Lebec, Wenov will have more than 23,000m2 to accommodate research centers, laboratories, showrooms, coworking spaces but also schools and training centers open to all generations. A new type of residence will be able to accommodate students but also visiting researchers and professionals from all over the world. The living and working environment will be expanded with catering spaces, a day nursery and many innovative spaces in the heart of this new eco-district.

A meeting point for meetings and innovations
This living space will be, from 2020, a new essential meeting point for the Lille and European tech ecosystem, ideally located between Paris, London and Brussels. As for architecture and environmental biases, innovation must develop in open mode, to connect all talents. This new location will make it easier and more long-term for all the players involved in Euratechnologies to collaborate with one motto: training, sharing and co-construction of our technological and economic future.
Ideally located in the heart of the metropolis, this real urban laboratory at the service of technological innovation is a bet on the future. A bet on the major challenge of training citizens, young and old, throughout life, in digital issues. The goal is to build an inclusive digital culture hub that is open to the masses. With the creation of 2 schools and 2 training centers, but also with the proximity of coworking spaces and fablabs to encourage experimentation and the test and learn specific to the emergence of innovation.
A vision and strong environmental commitments
For Jean-Philippe Lebecq, from Lalou + Lebec Architectes, "On Wenov, innovation is considered in open mode: our architectural bias reflects the desire to develop and promote digital culture." This signature of "building city, building landscape [...] fluid and welcoming" should also be responsible and "eco-friendly". The approach of the architectural firm and Vinci Immobilier is based on strong social principles, as we have seen, but also on respect for the environment and nature. Environmental excellence is a strong pillar of the Wenov project. Between city and nature, this new crossroads of innovation is part of Vinci Immobilier's sustainable real estate initiatives, with strict respect for energy consumption, water treatment and waste as well as societal criteria of health, well-being and accessibility of transport.

In this environmental approach, several labels certify the actions of the project bearers in the long term:

  • The NF Habitat label for global issues of sustainable construction
  • The "Carbon Energy" label for energy performance and carbon impact during construction and occupation
  • The Biodivercity label to encourage actions on the ground between inhabitants and residents
